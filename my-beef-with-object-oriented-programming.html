My Beef with Object-Oriented Programming

May 07, 2015

Summary: * *

We've all seen it: a programmer shows you the code she has worked on. She's beaming with pride. You look at it, and it's purely structural. A.main() calls A.start() which constructs a B and calls B.initialize() (empty method), then creates a C and calls C.run(). C.run() is an empty method. So many hard decisions have all been made: the class hierarchy is set, the responsibilities are determined, and the call chain is perfected. All of that work, and yet it does virtually nothing.

I have a serious beef with Object-Oriented Programming as I see it practiced, as people write about it, and as people give presentations about it. The culture of Object-Oriented Programming is all about where code lives. It's the decision that is most often debated in OOP. It's due to the nature of OO languages--the means of expression they give the programmer. The discussion of where code belongs distracts from the actual work of programming.
